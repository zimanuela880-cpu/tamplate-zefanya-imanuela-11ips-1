Avatar: Fire and Ash

05-Feb-2020 | Admin | Avatar: Fire and Ash

Avatar: Fire and Ash returns to Pandora as Jake Sully, Neytiri, and their family face rising tensions with humans and the fire-wielding Ash People, a dangerous Na’vi clan led by Varang. Set shortly after The Way of Water, the film blends breathtaking visuals, expanded world-building, and intense action with themes of grief, moral ambiguity, and cultural conflict. Critics praise its spectacle and immersive scope, though note its lengthy runtime and familiar story beats.

Shortly after settling in with the Metkayina, Jake Sully and his family grieve over Neteyam's death.[b] Neytiri has developed hatred toward humans. When a fleet of flying merchant ships arrive, Jake and Neytiri decide Spider must leave the Metkayina and return to the human scientist camp, but that the family will accompany him for the trip.

The ship convoy is ambushed by the volcano-dwelling and warmongering Mangkwan, an aggressive Na'vi tribe who reject Eywa. Led by their matriarch Varang, the Mangkwan set fire to the ships, looting them and killing many. The family is separated. Colonel Miles Quaritch and Corporal Lyle Wainfleet find Jake, and they reluctantly work together to find Spider. Spider's oxygen mask loses power, and he collapses from asphyxiation. Kiri connects her queue to the ground and goes into a trance. After his body is infused with mycelia, Spider gains the ability to breathe Pandora's atmosphere.

Eventually, the children are trapped by the Mangkwan. Quaritch and Jake arrive but are captured, and Quaritch teaches Varang how to use guns. Kiri commands the flora to kill their guards, enabling their escape. After examining Spider, Norm Spellman and Max Patel discover an organism inside his body, which they realize could be reverse engineered to allow every human to breathe on Pandora. He has also grown a neural queue on his head, allowing him to connect to Pandoran fauna like the Na'vi. Kiri learns that she was sired by Eywa, but is blocked from connecting with her. Quaritch travels to the Mangkwan's village, bringing them supplies of firearms and flamethrowers. He convinces them to form an alliance, entering a romantic relationship with Varang
